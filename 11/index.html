Biography:

Marie Curie was one of the greatest scientists of all time.
 She was born on November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland. 
 Her full name was Maria Skłodowska-Curie, but the world knows her as Marie Curie.
      
First Woman to Win a Nobel Prize:
      Marie Curie was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize. 
      She won it twice—once in Physics (1903) and once in Chemistry (1911). 
      No one else has ever won Nobel Prizes in two different sciences!
      
Discoveries:
        She discovered two new elements:
        Polonium (named after her homeland, Poland) and Radium.
        Her work on radioactivity changed science forever.
        She even coined the term "radioactivity."
        
Helping Humanity:
      During World War I, Marie Curie invented mobile X-ray machines to help doctors treat wounded soldiers.
      She also trained nurses to use these machines, saving countless lives.
    
Her Struggles:
        Marie faced many challenges in her life.
        She was a woman in a time when women were not allowed to study or work in science.
        She worked in a small, cold lab with very little money.
        She lost her husband,Pierre Curie , in a tragic accident, but she continued her work despite her grief.

~Thank you, Marie Curie, for your incredible contributions to science and humanity. You will always be remembered as a true hero.
